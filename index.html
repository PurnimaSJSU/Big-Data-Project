Abstract

The purpose of the project is to analyze a dataset for used cars in the United States of America and understand the variation in prices and depreciation over time for different car models on the American market. The dataset details various features for pre-owned cars. These features help structure the analysis and define the significant factors that are involved in the best purchase decision for used cars. The dataset is a collection of several tables from different sources. All the information represented is related to used cars across different states in the United States. The first source has the information of 3 million cars used between 1915 and 2021, extracted from the Cargurus inventory in September 2020. The table consists of 66 columns, detailing the carsâ€™ architecture, engine, fuel consumption, and year listed on the market, etc. The second source outlines around 430,000 used cars information posted for sale on Craigslist and provides data related to the price, brand, and year of manufacturing among 23 other categories. The third and final source gathered presents the sale of around 160,000 used cars between 2019 and 2020 on eBay. The columns represent the model of the car, the price, the mileage, the location as well as other details related to the car.
